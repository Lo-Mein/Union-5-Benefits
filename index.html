Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions from medical sales professionals about managing finances as a 1099 contractor

What makes Union 5 Benefits different from other financial advisors?

We specialize exclusively in working with 1099 medical sales professionals. Our team includes former medical sales reps who understand the unique compensation structures, career paths, and financial challenges you face. Unlike general financial advisors, we design all our strategies around the specific needs of independent contractors in medical sales.

How can I save for retirement when my income fluctuates month to month?

We develop retirement strategies designed specifically for variable income. This includes using percentage-based contribution approaches rather than fixed dollar amounts, implementing "catch-up" strategies during high-commission months, and leveraging both the employer and employee contribution options available to self-employed individuals.

What healthcare options are available to me as a 1099 contractor?

We provide access to several healthcare solutions including ACA marketplace plans, health-sharing ministries, association health plans, and ICHRA arrangements. Additionally, we help you maximize the tax benefits of HSAs and structure your business to potentially qualify for additional healthcare deductions not available to W-2 employees.

How do you help with tax planning for commission-based income?

Our tax strategies include quarterly planning to prevent underpayment penalties, identifying all available business deductions for 1099 contractors, implementing entity structures when beneficial, managing income timing strategies, and maximizing retirement contributions to reduce taxable income. We work proactively throughout the year, not just at tax time.

Can I qualify for a mortgage with variable 1099 income?

Yes! We work with lenders who specialize in mortgages for self-employed professionals. We help you prepare the proper documentation to verify your income, structure your finances to improve debt-to-income ratios, and time your application strategically. Many of our clients have successfully purchased homes despite having commission-based income.
